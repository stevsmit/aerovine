    AeroVine Launches to Revolutionize Vineyard Management with Advanced Drone Technology¶

    Napa Valley, California 12-2-2025

    AeroVine, a new startup specializing in agricultural drone solutions, today announced its official launch, aiming to transform how vineyards monitor and manage their crops. AeroVine's cutting-edge drone services provide vineyard owners and winemakers with unprecedented precision and data insights to optimize vine health, improve yield, and ensure sustainability.

    AeroVine: Precision Viticulture Takes Flight¶

    AeroVine's core offering centers on high-resolution aerial imagery and proprietary analytical software designed specifically for the unique challenges of viticulture. Traditional vineyard monitoring is labor-intensive and often relies on broad assumptions. AeroVine's technology overcomes these limitations by delivering detailed, plant-level data, including:

    • Vigor Mapping: Using multispectral and Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) analysis to identify variations in vine health across the block.

    • Water Stress Detection: Pinpointing specific areas suffering from dehydration, allowing for targeted irrigation adjustments.

    • Pest and Disease Identification: Early detection of infestations or fungal outbreaks before they spread widely.

    • Canopy Management Analysis: Assessing canopy density and leaf area index (LAI) for optimal sun exposure and grape quality.

    "The wine industry has always been a blend of tradition and science, but the 'science' side is about to get a major upgrade," said Shauna Diaz, CEO of AeroVine. "We are not just selling drone flights; we are providing actionable, geo-referenced intelligence that helps winemakers make better, faster decisions. Our technology is designed to save water, reduce pesticide use, and ultimately, produce a superior vintage."

    Strategic Partnership and Pilot Programs¶

    AeroVine has successfully completed pilot programs with several regional vineyards, demonstrating significant improvements in efficiency and grape quality.

    "Working with AeroVine has been a game-changer for our Cabernet Sauvignon blocks. The data allowed us to identify subtle irrigation issues we couldn't see from the ground and adjust our strategy in time. This kind of precision is the future of fine winemaking," said Henry Partner from Henry's Vineyard.

    The company is currently accepting new clients for its Summer 2025 service package and plans to focus on Willamette Valley and Finger Lakes before expanding nationally.

    About AeroVine¶

    Founded in 2025, AeroVine is a drone technology startup dedicated to advancing precision agriculture in the viticulture industry. The company utilizes state-of-the-art unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), multispectral sensors, and AI-driven data processing to provide vineyard managers with the most accurate, actionable information possible. AeroVine is committed to promoting sustainable practices and maximizing the quality and yield of premium grapes.
