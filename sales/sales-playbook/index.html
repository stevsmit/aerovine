    AeroVine Sales Playbook¶

    Customer Personas¶

    Boutique Vineyard Owner (10–100 acres)¶

    Name & Title¶

    Elena Moretti — Proprietor, Moretti Family Estate

    Vineyard Size & Wine Price Point¶

    • 60 acres under vine
    • Premium, small-lot wines ($35–$75/bottle)

    Annual Revenue Range¶

    $1–5 million

    Top 3 Pain Points¶

    1. Labor shortages during critical times (bud break, veraison, harvest) leading to delayed scouting.
    2. Disease detection (especially powdery mildew and botrytis) is often reactive rather than proactive.
    3. High variability in small vineyard blocks, making manual monitoring slow and inconsistent.

    Current Methods for Monitoring Vine Health¶

    • Weekly ATV/walking rows
    • Occasional third-party agronomist inspections
    • Basic soil moisture probes and a few weather sensors

    Budget Authority & Buying Process¶

    • As owner, sole decision-maker; decisions made quickly if ROI is clear.
    • Typically evaluates purchases >$10K with her accountant and winemaker.
    • Prefers simple pricing, low maintenance, and minimal training requirements.

    What Makes Them Say "Yes"¶

    • Clear evidence that drones reduce time spent scouting by 50%+
    • Ability to spot mildew pressure early and reduce fungicide sprays
    • A package that is easy to use, with training included, and doesn't require a dedicated technician

    Preferred Communication Style¶

    • Informal, story-driven, practical
    • Responds to short emails, demos, and case studies from similar family-run estates.
    • Appreciates clear ROI examples rather than technical jargon.

    Key Objections¶

    • "This seems too technical for my team."
    • "Is this worth it for only 60 acres?"
    • "I don't have time to learn new software."

    Mid-Size Winery Operations Manager (100–500 acres)¶

    Name & Title¶

    Carlos Ramirez — Operations Manager, Sierra Crest Vineyards

    Vineyard Size & Wine Price Point¶

    • 250 acres
    • Estate wines priced at $20–$50/bottle

    Annual Revenue Range¶

    $10–25 million

    Top 3 Pain Points¶

    1. Yield variability across blocks impacting forecasting accuracy.
    2. Escalating input costs (water, fertilizer, labor) with pressure to increase efficiency.
    3. Lack of real-time visibility into vine stress, leading to inconsistent irrigation decisions.

    Current Methods for Monitoring Vine Health¶

    • Field techs perform daily scouting across several blocks
    • Satellite NDVI imagery monthly (delayed and lower resolution)
    • Irrigation monitoring with distributed sensors but minimal aerial perspective

    Budget Authority & Buying Process¶

    • Manages a capex/opex budget up to ~$150K; requires GM approval for purchases >$30K
    • Buys technology on a pilot → evaluate → expand cycle
    • Expects integration with existing farm-management software

    What Makes Them Say "Yes"¶

    • Demonstrated ability to reduce water use, optimize irrigation, and improve block uniformity
    • Solid case studies from similar 200–500 acre wineries
    • Clear plan for training field staff and easy integration with current workflows
    • Ability to generate yield maps and actionable recommendations

    Preferred Communication Style¶

    • Data-driven, structured, and professional
    • Likes 15–20 minute briefings with graphs, dashboards, and KPIs
    • Responds positively to onsite demos during the growing season

    Key Objections¶

    • "Will this integrate with our vineyard management system?"
    • "Prove that drones give better insights than what my team already collects."
    • "This seems like another tool my staff won't consistently use."

    Large Corporate Vineyard Director (500+ acres)¶

    Name & Title¶

    Victoria Chen — Director of Viticulture, TerraVista Wine Group

    Vineyard Size & Wine Price Point¶

    • Oversees 1,500+ acres across multiple AVAs
    • Multiple product tiers: $15–$120/bottle, including reserve labels

    Annual Revenue Range¶

    $50–150 million

    Top 3 Pain Points¶

    1. Scale complexity — inconsistent vine performance across dozens of blocks and sites.
    2. Compliance & sustainability reporting requirements (water, pesticide, carbon) are time-consuming.
    3. Lack of standardized data across regions leading to sub-optimal resource allocation.

    Current Methods for Monitoring Vine Health¶

    • Regional vineyard managers with weekly scout reports
    • Contracted aerial imaging 2–4 times per year (slow turnaround, expensive)
    • High-end sensor networks, but data is siloed across systems

    Budget Authority & Buying Process¶

    • Controls a technology and innovation budget but requires CFO and COO approval for multi-site rollouts
    • Expects multi-year ROI models, compliance benefits, and scalability
    • Prefers vendors with enterprise support SLAs, API access, and governance documentation

    What Makes Them Say "Yes"¶

    • A drone platform that scales across regions and standardizes data collection
    • Ability to automate sustainability reporting and provide audit-ready documentation
    • Proven enterprise deployments and robust integration capabilities
    • Predictive models for disease pressure, yield forecasting, and irrigation optimization

    Preferred Communication Style¶

    • Executive-level, concise, strategic
    • Wants whitepapers, ROI models, system architecture diagrams
    • Prefers quarterly business reviews and pilot proof-of-value frameworks

    Key Objections¶

    • "Can your platform handle multi-site, multi-region operations?"
    • "We need guaranteed data security, compliance, and uptime."
    • "Show me ROI beyond pretty maps — what decisions improve and by how much?"

    IV. Top Pain Points (The Problems AeroVine Solves)¶

    AeroVine provides solutions to critical issues that directly impact the value of a premium vintage:

    1. Late Disease Detection: Missing early-stage diseases (like trunk diseases) until visible symptoms appear, leading to non-recoverable crop loss.
    2. Water/Input Inefficiency: Wasting resources by applying uniform irrigation/fungicide across variable blocks, which elevates costs and reduces grape quality.
    3. Lack of Quality Uniformity: High variability in ripening (Brix/Acidity) across a block, forcing difficult harvest decisions and lowering the quality grade of the final blend.
    4. Labor Cost/Scarcity: Reliance on expensive, highly-skilled manual labor for scouting and precise vineyard management tasks.
    5. Uncertain Yield Forecasting: Inability to accurately predict harvest tonnage and quality 60–90 days out, impacting sales contracts and winery capacity planning.

    Market Trends & Operating Environment¶

    Category Current Challenges Facing Vineyard Operators Technology Adoption Trends
    Challenges 1. Climate Volatility: Increasing frequency of extreme heat/drought events necessitates hyper-precise irrigation (VRDI). 2. Labor Crisis: Rising cost and scarcity of skilled labor for critical tasks (e.g., precise pruning, scouting). 3.     Growth Regions Top 5 Regions Experiencing Growth/Investment: Napa/Sonoma (CA), Willamette Valley (OR), Marlborough (NZ), Rhône Valley (France), Mendoza (Argentina) Regulations: Increasing scrutiny on water usage, pesticide drift, and labor safety/wage compliance. Drones offer auditable proof of precision application and resource efficiency.

    Key Diseases & Issues Drones Detect (The Cost Impact)¶

    AeroVine's multispectral and AI analysis provides early detection, which translates directly into cost mitigation and quality preservation.

    Issue Detected Cost Impact & Rationale for Premium Vineyards Solution Focus
    Water Stress Risk: Loss of uniformity in ripening (Brix/Acid), reducing wine quality grade. Early detection allows for Variable Rate Deficit Irrigation (VRDI) prescription maps, ensuring optimal stress levels for quality.
    Powdery Mildew Risk: If undetected early, infection can reduce yield by 50% and lower wine quality significantly across a block. Drone-guided Spot Treatment minimizes chemical use and prevents widespread outbreak, protecting investment.
    Grapevine Trunk Disease Risk: Systemic disease requires costly vine removal and re-planting, with a $15,000–$40,000 per-acre loss of production over 3-5 years. Enhanced spectral imaging detects sub-clinical symptoms before visual decline, isolating diseased vines for proactive removal.
    Red Blotch / Leaf Roll Virus Risk: Quality loss (poor color and flavor development) can drop a wine's tier classification, resulting in a $20–$50 loss per bottle. Mapping disease spread allows for targeted pruning/removal, protecting clean surrounding blocks and quality control.
    Pest Damage Risk: Localized damage from insects or birds can quickly destroy specific high-value rows. High-resolution mapping identifies damage hotspots, informing placement of deterrents or targeted biocontrol applications.

    Buying Season & Outreach Strategy¶

    Stage of Season When They Buy (Budget Cycle) Best Time for AeroVine Outreach
    Winter Primary Decision Window (Q4/Q1): Budgets finalized for the upcoming season; input costs (labor, chemicals) are set. December - February: Focus on ROI and Annual Contracts. Target the Ops Director with savings data.
    Pre-Harvest Urgent Problem Solving (Q3): Seeking immediate, high-value fixes to secure the vintage (e.g., ripeness uniformity mapping). July - August: Focus on Pilot Programs and Quality Assurance. Target the Vintner with quality elevation data.

    1. Unified Wine & Grape Symposium (Sacramento, CA): Premier North American event; mandatory for meeting U.S. West Coast decision-makers. (Jan/Feb)
    2. World Bulk Wine Exhibition (Amsterdam/Regional): Important for large corporate clients concerned with commodity pricing and global supply chain. (Nov/Dec)
    3. Vinitech-Sifel (Bordeaux, France): Leading international technology trade show focusing on viticulture and winemaking equipment. (Nov/Dec, Biennial)
    4. Oregon Wine Symposium (Portland, OR): Ideal for targeting premium, smaller Willamette Valley producers. (Feb)
    5. Grape & Wine Research & Development Conference (Australia/Varies): Excellent for meeting international viticultural consultants and technology thought leaders. (Varies)
