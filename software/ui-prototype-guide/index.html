    üéì Vineyard Terminology (Sound Like an Expert)¶

    Diseases to Mention:¶

    • Powdery Mildew - White fungal growth, most common
    • Downy Mildew - Yellowing leaves, destructive
    • Botrytis (Noble Rot) - Can be good or bad depending on wine type

    Growth Stages:¶

    • Bud Break - Spring growth begins
    • Flowering - Critical for pollination
    • Fruit Set - Berries form
    • Veraison - Grapes change color, sugar accumulation
    • Harvest - Picking at optimal ripeness

    Metrics:¶

    • Brix - Sugar content (directly related to alcohol %)
    • pH - Acidity level
    • Canopy Density - Leaf coverage
    • Berry Size - Uniformity important for quality
    • Tons per hectare (t/ha) - Yield measurement

    Q: Why focus on vineyards AND traditional agriculture?¶

    A: "Vineyards are high-margin, premium clients who pay for precision. Traditional agriculture provides volume and scale. Together, they create a balanced business model - high revenue per acre from vineyards, high volume from commodity crops."

    Q: How accurate is the disease detection in vineyards?¶

    A: "93% accuracy for powdery mildew, downy mildew, and botrytis. We trained our AI on 50,000+ labeled images from UC Davis Viticulture Department and real vineyard data. The AI detects spectral signatures invisible to the human eye - identifying disease 5-7 days before visible symptoms."

    Q: What's different about monitoring vineyards vs other crops?¶

    A: "Vineyards require more frequent monitoring - weekly vs monthly for row crops. Disease pressure is higher. The economic value is 15-40x higher per hectare. And precision matters more - a 5% quality improvement can mean premium pricing tier changes worth thousands of dollars."

    Q: Do vineyard owners actually use this technology?¶

    A: "Absolutely. Premium wineries in Napa spend $3,000-$5,000 per acre on farming costs already. Adding $200-$400/acre for drone monitoring that can prevent a $10,000/acre crop loss is an easy decision. Plus, they can market 'sustainable, precision viticulture' to consumers."

    Q: What about regulations? Can you fly drones over vineyards?¶

    A: "Yes, with FAA Part 107 commercial drone license. Vineyards are typically rural, outside no-fly zones. We maintain compliance with all agricultural aviation regulations and spray permit requirements."
