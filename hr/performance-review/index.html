    AeroVine Performance Review Framework¶

    Designed for Fast-Growth Startup Environment¶

    Overview¶

    AeroVine's performance review framework is designed to support rapid growth while maintaining high performance standards. This framework emphasizes continuous feedback, goal alignment, and professional development in a fast-paced startup environment.

    Key Principles: - Continuous Feedback: Regular check-ins, not just annual reviews - Goal Alignment: Individual goals tied to company objectives - Growth Mindset: Focus on development and learning - Transparency: Clear expectations and evaluation criteria - Agility: Framework adapts as company scales

    Review Cycle Structure¶

    Quarterly Performance Reviews¶

    • Frequency: Every 3 months
    • Duration: 30-45 minutes
    • Format: 1:1 meeting with manager
    • Focus: Goal progress, feedback, development planning

    Annual Performance Review¶

    • Frequency: Once per year (typically Q4)
    • Duration: 60-90 minutes
    • Format: Comprehensive review with manager + self-assessment
    • Focus: Year-end evaluation, compensation review, career planning

    Continuous Check-Ins¶

    • Frequency: Bi-weekly 1:1s (minimum)
    • Duration: 30 minutes
    • Format: Informal conversation
    • Focus: Day-to-day feedback, blockers, support needs

    Performance Dimensions¶

    1. Results & Impact (40%)¶

    What: Achievement of goals and measurable outcomes

    Evaluation Criteria: - Goal completion rate - Quality of deliverables - Impact on business metrics - Customer/client satisfaction - Revenue contribution (for revenue-generating roles)

    Examples: - Engineering: Features shipped, bug reduction, system uptime - Sales: Revenue targets, pipeline growth, customer acquisition - Operations: Service delivery quality, customer retention, efficiency metrics - Product: Feature adoption, user satisfaction, roadmap execution

    Rating Scale: - Exceeds Expectations (4): Consistently exceeds goals, delivers exceptional results - Meets Expectations (3): Meets all goals, delivers quality work - Partially Meets (2): Meets some goals, needs improvement - Below Expectations (1): Fails to meet goals, significant gaps

    2. Technical/Functional Competency (25%)¶

    What: Mastery of role-specific skills and knowledge

    Evaluation Criteria: - Technical skills and expertise - Problem-solving ability - Quality of work output - Innovation and creativity - Industry knowledge

    Examples: - Engineering: Code quality, architecture decisions, technical leadership - Sales: Sales methodology, product knowledge, negotiation skills - Operations: Flight operations expertise, safety compliance, efficiency - Product: Product strategy, user research, market understanding

    Rating Scale: - Expert (4): Recognized expert, mentors others, drives innovation - Proficient (3): Strong skills, independent contributor - Developing (2): Building skills, needs guidance - Needs Development (1): Significant skill gaps, requires training

    3. Collaboration & Communication (20%)¶

    What: Effectiveness in working with others and communicating

    Evaluation Criteria: - Team collaboration - Cross-functional communication - Conflict resolution - Knowledge sharing - Customer/client interaction

    Examples: - Active participation in team meetings - Clear and timely communication - Constructive feedback to peers - Documentation and knowledge sharing - Customer relationship management

    Rating Scale: - Exceptional (4): Excellent collaborator, strong communicator, builds relationships - Effective (3): Good collaborator, clear communication - Adequate (2): Basic collaboration, communication needs improvement - Needs Improvement (1): Poor collaboration, communication issues

    4. Growth & Development (10%)¶

    What: Commitment to learning and professional growth

    Evaluation Criteria: - Proactive learning - Skill development - Feedback receptiveness - Adaptability to change - Career development initiative

    Examples: - Takes on stretch assignments - Seeks feedback and acts on it - Learns new technologies/methods - Adapts to company changes - Sets and pursues development goals

    Rating Scale: - Exemplary (4): Highly proactive, rapid growth, mentors others - Strong (3): Actively develops, open to feedback - Moderate (2): Some development, needs encouragement - Limited (1): Minimal growth, resistant to feedback

    5. Culture & Values Alignment (5%)¶

    What: Demonstration of AeroVine's values and culture

    AeroVine Values: - Innovation: Embracing new ideas and experimentation - Safety First: Prioritizing safety in all operations - Customer Focus: Deep commitment to customer success - Growth Mindset: Continuous learning and improvement - Integrity: Honest, ethical, and transparent

    Evaluation Criteria: - Embodies company values - Contributes to positive culture - Acts with integrity - Supports company mission

    Rating Scale: - Exemplary (4): Role model for values, culture champion - Strong (3): Consistently demonstrates values - Adequate (2): Generally aligns with values - Needs Improvement (1): Values misalignment concerns

    Goal Setting Framework (OKRs)¶

    Objective and Key Results (OKRs)¶

    Each employee sets 3-5 OKRs per quarter, aligned with company objectives.

    Structure: - Objective: Qualitative, inspirational goal (1-2 sentences) - Key Results: 2-3 measurable outcomes (quantitative metrics)

    Example - Software Engineer: - Objective: Improve platform reliability and performance - Key Results: - Achieve 99.9% uptime for core services - Reduce average API response time by 20% - Deploy automated testing covering 80% of codebase

    Example - Sales Manager: - Objective: Expand customer base in Sonoma County - Key Results: - Acquire 15 new vineyard customers - Generate $200K in new revenue - Achieve 90% customer satisfaction score

    Goal Alignment¶

    • Individual goals support department goals
    • Department goals support company goals
    • Reviewed and adjusted quarterly

    Review Process¶

    Step 1: Self-Assessment (1 week before review)¶

    Employee completes self-assessment covering: - Goal achievement summary - Key accomplishments - Challenges faced - Areas for development - Career goals and interests

    Step 2: Manager Preparation (3-5 days before review)¶

    Manager prepares: - Goal progress analysis - Performance observations - Feedback from peers/customers - Development recommendations - Compensation considerations (annual review)

    Step 3: Review Meeting¶

    Agenda: 1. Review goals and achievements (15 min) 2. Discuss performance dimensions (20 min) 3. Provide feedback and recognition (10 min) 4. Identify development areas (10 min) 5. Set next quarter goals (15 min) 6. Career discussion (10 min)

    Step 4: Documentation¶

    • Review summary documented in HRIS
    • Goals updated for next quarter
    • Development plan created/updated
    • Action items assigned

    Step 5: Follow-Up¶

    • 30-day check-in on development plan
    • Ongoing support and coaching
    • Quarterly goal progress tracking

    Performance Ratings & Outcomes¶

    Overall Performance Rating¶

    Calculation: - Weighted average of all dimensions - Manager discretion for final rating - Calibration with leadership team

    Rating Distribution Guidelines¶

    • Exceeds Expectations (4): Top 10-15% of performers
    • Meets Expectations (3): 70-80% of employees
    • Partially Meets (2): 10-15% of employees
    • Below Expectations (1): <5% of employees

    Outcomes by Rating¶

    Exceeds Expectations (4.0)¶

    • Recognition: Public acknowledgment, spot bonus consideration
    • Compensation: Top-tier merit increase, bonus eligibility
    • Development: Stretch assignments, leadership opportunities
    • Retention: High priority for retention, promotion consideration

    Meets Expectations (3.0)¶

    • Recognition: Positive feedback, continued support
    • Compensation: Standard merit increase, bonus eligibility
    • Development: Standard development opportunities
    • Retention: Normal retention priority

    Partially Meets (2.0)¶

    • Recognition: Constructive feedback, improvement plan
    • Compensation: Limited/no merit increase
    • Development: Performance improvement plan (PIP)
    • Retention: 90-day improvement period, close monitoring

    Below Expectations (1.0)¶

    • Recognition: Formal performance improvement plan
    • Compensation: No merit increase
    • Development: Intensive coaching, specific improvement targets
    • Retention: 30-60 day improvement period, termination risk

    Development Planning¶

    Individual Development Plan (IDP)¶

    Created during annual review, updated quarterly.

    Components: 1. Current State: Skills, strengths, gaps 2. Future State: Career goals, desired skills 3. Development Activities: - On-the-job learning (70%) - Coaching/mentoring (20%) - Formal training (10%) 4. Timeline: 6-12 month plan 5. Success Metrics: How progress will be measured

    Development Opportunities¶

    • Stretch Assignments: Challenging projects outside comfort zone
    • Cross-Functional Projects: Exposure to other departments
    • Mentorship: Pair with senior team member
    • Training: Courses, conferences, certifications
    • Job Shadowing: Learn from other roles
    • Leadership Opportunities: Lead projects or initiatives

    Feedback Mechanisms¶

    360-Degree Feedback (Annual)¶

    • Peer feedback
    • Direct report feedback (for managers)
    • Customer/client feedback (where applicable)
    • Self-assessment

    Real-Time Feedback¶

    • Immediate feedback on projects
    • Weekly/bi-weekly 1:1s
    • Project retrospectives
    • Team feedback sessions

    Anonymous Feedback¶

    • Quarterly pulse surveys
    • Culture and engagement surveys
    • Suggestion box/feedback channel

    Calibration Process¶

    Quarterly Calibration (Leadership Team)¶

    • Review all performance ratings
    • Ensure consistency across departments
    • Identify high performers and development needs
    • Discuss promotion and compensation decisions

    Calibration Criteria¶

    • Rating distribution alignment
    • Consistency in evaluation standards
    • Identification of bias or inconsistencies
    • Fairness and equity considerations

    Special Considerations for Fast-Growth Startups¶

    Rapid Role Evolution¶

    • Goals may shift quarterly as company pivots
    • Flexibility in evaluation criteria
    • Emphasis on adaptability and learning

    Limited Historical Data¶

    • Focus on recent performance (last 3-6 months)
    • Qualitative feedback alongside metrics
    • Emphasis on trajectory and potential

    Resource Constraints¶

    • Efficient review process (not overly bureaucratic)
    • Focus on high-impact feedback
    • Leverage technology for automation

    High-Performance Culture¶

    • Clear expectations and accountability
    • Fast feedback loops
    • Rapid course correction
    • Recognition of contributions

    Tools & Technology¶

    Performance Management System¶

    • HRIS Integration: Centralized employee records
    • Goal Tracking: OKR software (e.g., 15Five, Lattice, Culture Amp)
    • Feedback Tools: Real-time feedback platforms
    • Analytics: Performance trends and insights

    Documentation¶

    • Review summaries stored in HRIS
    • Goals tracked in OKR system
    • Development plans documented
    • Feedback logged for historical reference

    Manager Training¶

    Required Training for Managers¶

    • Performance review best practices
    • Giving constructive feedback
    • Goal setting and OKRs
    • Development planning
    • Difficult conversations
    • Bias awareness and mitigation

    Ongoing Support¶

    • Manager coaching and support
    • Regular manager meetings
    • Best practice sharing
    • Access to HR for guidance

    Employee Resources¶

    Self-Service Resources¶

    • Performance review guide
    • Goal-setting templates
    • Development planning worksheets
    • Career development resources

    Support Channels¶

    • Manager 1:1s
    • HR consultation
    • Career coaching (as available)
    • Learning and development programs

    Review Schedule Example¶

    Q1 Review (January)¶

    • Review Q4 performance
    • Set Q1 goals
    • Annual review preparation (if applicable)

    Q2 Review (April)¶

    • Review Q1 performance
    • Set Q2 goals
    • Mid-year check-in

    Q3 Review (July)¶

    • Review Q2 performance
    • Set Q3 goals
    • Development plan update

    Q4 Review (October)¶

    • Review Q3 performance
    • Annual performance review
    • Set Q1 goals for next year
    • Compensation review
    • Career planning discussion

    Success Metrics¶

    Framework Effectiveness¶

    • Employee satisfaction with review process
    • Goal achievement rates
    • Time to complete reviews
    • Manager confidence in process
    • Employee engagement scores

    Performance Outcomes¶

    • High performer retention
    • Improvement in underperformers
    • Promotion rates
    • Skill development progress
    • Business goal achievement

    Continuous Improvement¶

    Quarterly Review of Framework¶

    • Gather feedback from employees and managers
    • Identify process improvements
    • Update templates and guidelines
    • Adjust for company growth stage

    Annual Framework Review¶

    • Comprehensive evaluation
    • Benchmark against best practices
    • Major updates and improvements
    • Training and communication updates

    This framework is a living document and will evolve as AeroVine grows. Feedback and suggestions are welcome from all employees.
