### **Q: How does the AI actually detect diseases?**
**A:** "We use convolutional neural networks trained on 50,000+ labeled images from UC Davis Viticulture Department and real vineyard data. The AI analyzes multispectral imagery - specifically the near-infrared and red-edge bands that show chlorophyll content. Diseased plants have different spectral signatures than healthy ones. For powdery mildew specifically, we look for reduced reflectance in the 700-750nm range, which indicates fungal interference with photosynthesis. The AI detects these changes 5-7 days before visible symptoms."

**Why this answer works:** Technically detailed, references real agricultural research, explains the "why" not just "what."

---

### **Q: What about privacy? Farmers won't want their data shared.**
**A:** "Excellent question. Our API uses OAuth 2.0 with granular permissions - farmers control exactly what data is shared and with whom. All data is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). We're SOC 2 compliant and designed for GDPR readiness. Each farm's data is completely siloed - Vineyard A cannot see Vineyard B's data. We never sell data to third parties. The farmer owns their data, period."

**Why this answer works:** Addresses concern directly, mentions specific standards, reassures control remains with customer.

---

### **Q: Why would vineyard owners trust a drone over their own eyes?**

We've found 87% of our detections happen 5-7 days before visible symptoms. Early detection gives them options - preventative spray costs less than emergency treatment."

**Why this answer works:** Positions as tool, not replacement. Uses "augmented intelligence" buzzword correctly. Data-backed (87%, 5-7 days).

### **Q: What about weather - can drones fly in all conditions?**
**A:** "No, and that's actually a feature. Our API includes real-time weather integration. The system automatically delays missions if wind exceeds 15 km/h or rain is forecast within 2 hours. This isn't a limitation - it's regulatory compliance and safety. For spraying operations specifically, you WANT calm conditions or the chemicals drift off-target. ### **Q: How is this different from satellite imagery?**
**A:** "Satellites are excellent for large-scale trends - we actually use satellite data to prioritize which fields need drone surveys. But satellites have limitations: 10-meter resolution vs our 5-centimeter resolution, 5-7 day revisit time vs our on-demand daily flights, and cloud cover blocks satellites entirely. For vineyards specifically, we need to see individual vine rows to pinpoint which block has disease. Satellites can tell you 'something's wrong in the south half of your vineyard' - we tell you 'Block 7, rows 12-18, powdery mildew, treat immediately.'"

**Why this answer works:** Acknowledges satellite value, explains specific advantages, concrete example shows practical difference.
